## Week 1: Foundations of AI and Prompt Engineering

### Lesson 1.1: Introduction to AI and Large Language Models
**Duration**: 4 hours | **Week**: 1 | **Day**: Monday AM

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:
1. Define artificial intelligence, machine learning, and large language models
2. Explain how LLMs process and generate text
3. Identify capabilities and limitations of modern AI systems
4. Recognize common causes of AI hallucinations

#### Lesson Structure

**Hour 1: What is AI?**
- Introduction to artificial intelligence concepts (20 min)
- Machine learning vs. traditional programming (20 min)
- Evolution from rule-based systems to neural networks (20 min)

**Hour 2: Understanding Large Language Models**
- Architecture of LLMs (transformers, attention mechanisms) (30 min)
- Training process and data requirements (30 min)

**Hour 3: Capabilities of Modern AI**
- Text generation and completion (15 min)
- Code generation and analysis (15 min)
- Data analysis and summarization (15 min)
- Multimodal capabilities (15 min)

**Hour 4: Limitations and Hallucinations**
- Understanding AI hallucinations and their causes (30 min)
- Knowledge cutoff dates and outdated information (15 min)
- Context window limitations (15 min)

#### Practical Activities
- Experiment with different AI models to observe outputs
- Document examples of hallucinations
- Create comparison chart of AI capabilities vs. limitations

#### Assessment
- Write 500-word summary of AI capabilities and limitations
- Include 3 real-world examples of each

#### Resources
- Prompt Engineering Guide - Introduction
- Learn Prompting - Getting Started
- IBM's 2025 Guide to Prompt Engineering

## Teaching Methodology

### Learning Approach
- **Theory to Practice**: Every lesson combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice
- **Iterative Learning**: Concepts are revisited and deepened throughout the course
- **Real-World Application**: All exercises use realistic business scenarios
- **Self-Directed**: Designed for independent study with clear guidance

### Assessment Strategy
- **Formative Assessment**: Ongoing through practical activities
- **Summative Assessment**: Weekly deliverables and final capstone
- **Self-Assessment**: Regular reflection and self-evaluation
- **Practical Demonstration**: Skills proven through working implementations

### Time Management
- **50-10 Rule**: 50 minutes study, 10 minutes break
- **Daily Goals**: Clear objectives for each study session
- **Weekly Reviews**: Consolidation at end of each week
- **Flexibility**: Adjust pacing based on individual progress
